# Zambezi And Futaleufu Photoreal Goal

## Objective

Expand the active photoreal environment goal so the fourth and fifth runnable rivers are:

- Zambezi River, Batoka Gorge: a photoreal big-water basalt gorge run below Victoria Falls.
- Futaleufu River, Chile: a photoreal Patagonian turquoise big-water run with boulder gardens and forested Andean valley walls.

The Codex goal tracker is still occupied by the original South Fork American, Grand Canyon Colorado, and Pacuare objective. This note is the source-controlled expansion until the active tracker can be replaced or completed.

## Shared Promotion Gates

Neither river can be called lifelike, production-playable, or screenshot-ready until these gates are attached and reviewed:

- Source manifests for terrain, hydrography, access/publication constraints, protected areas, and route stationing.
- Seasonal or release/rain/snowmelt flow bands with source, units, time zone, station relation, and guide review.
- Aerial/satellite imagery or first-party field capture plans with provider, acquisition date, CRS, terms, and processing provenance.
- Link-only public/social/reference media queues, with no downloaded or promoted third-party media until item-level rights are explicit.
- First-party or rights-cleared procedural material, foliage, rock, mist, foam, water, and atmosphere recipes.
- Unreal preview maps, guide-seat captures, river-eye captures, automated capture-quality review, human lifelike review, and desktop/VR performance evidence.
- Validation that visual/feature forcing is bounded, manifest-recorded, flow-dependent, GeoClaw/C++ compared where relevant, and not hiding conservation or raft-coupling failures.

## Zambezi River, Batoka Gorge

Target the Batoka Gorge character first: steep dark basalt walls, constricted canyon scale, warm spray and mist, strong wave trains, laterals, boils, holes, surging eddy lines, and flow-dependent hole stickiness or washout. Gameplay tuning must support commercial big-water rafting lines while preserving visibility for swimmers, pins, flips, rescues, and high-side timing.

Initial data plan:

- Identify authoritative terrain/DEM candidates for the Batoka Gorge corridor and record CRS/terms before import.
- Review Zambezi gauge or release/flow-history sources that can define low, normal runnable, high, and washout-style visual bands.
- Build link-only reference queues for official tourism/park/outfitter pages, guide footage leads, and public media without downloading or training on third-party assets.
- Add protected-area, access, cross-border Zambia/Zimbabwe publication, and sensitive-location review before public screenshots.
- Use procedural basalt strata, wet rock, plunge mist, canyon haze, warm river color, foam, and spray as first-party placeholders until source-conditioned or approved assets replace them.

## Futaleufu River, Chile

Target the Futaleufu character first: clear turquoise water, high-energy wave trains, granite or metamorphic boulder gardens, steep forested valley walls, gravel bars, Andean background, and strong seasonal variation from snowmelt and rain. Gameplay tuning must support powerful hydraulics, boulder pushes, pins/releases, flips, swimmer drift, and rescue readability.

Initial data plan:

- Identify Chilean official terrain, hydrography, protected-area, access, and hydrology source candidates with terms and CRS recorded before import.
- Define planning flow bands for low, normal runnable, high, and unsafe/flood-style looks, with station-to-reach relation and guide/outfitter review.
- Build link-only reference queues for official regional sources, guide/outfitter media leads, field-capture requests, and public/social pages without importing third-party media.
- Use procedural turquoise water depth, wet granite/boulder surfaces, temperate rainforest/valley foliage, snowmelt atmosphere, foam, and mist as first-party placeholders until approved assets replace them.
- Preserve hazard readability for big-water features, shorelines, swimmers, rescue targets, and raft contact outcomes while art quality improves.
